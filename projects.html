University

Top Shopper

This game was created as the term long project for CPSC 585 (Games Programming) at the University of Calgary. Our group consisted of five students. We had to develop a game fitting the racing genre. The game was built for Windows in Visual Studio 2017 using C++, GLSL, OpenGL, GLFW, glad, GLM, stb, FreeType, PhysX, and SDL2.

Mouse Racer

My first Blender animation created for CPSC 587 (Fundamentals of Computer Animation) at the University of Calgary.

WaveTool

C++/OpenGL tool for demonstrating Real-Time Rendering of Ocean Water With Dynamic Skybox. It was originally developed as the term long individual project for CPSC 591 (Rendering) at the University of Calgary. The project was developed following several milestones (proposal, progress reports, demo/presentation, and final report).

CageTool

C++/OpenGL tool for demonstrating Cage Generation for Mean-Value Deformation. It was originally developed as the term long group project for CPSC 589 (Modelling for Computer Graphics) at the University of Calgary. Our group consisted of three students with me acting as the lead programmer / project manager. The project was developed following several milestones (pitch, proposal, demos, and final paper).

paper

Arkanoid

Arkanoid game clone written in ARMv7 Assembly to run on a Raspberry Pi 3 Model B with SNES controller input. It was created as an individual assignment for CPSC 359 (Computing Machinery II) at the University of Calgary.

Cookbook

C#/WPF/XAML prototype of a cooking instructor application, with the purpose of assisting people with learning, preparing, and cooking a recipe. The interface was designed and implemented by following the Task-Centered System Design (TCSD) methodology. It was originally developed as the fall 2018 term long group project for CPSC 481 (Human-Computer Interaction I) at the University of Calgary. Our group consisted of five students.
